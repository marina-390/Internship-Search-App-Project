Resume Building Tips

We help you make a simple resume for your first internship

📄

Basic Structure

📏

Keep it 1 Page

Focus on most relevant info for internships. Recruiters spend ~7 seconds scanning.

🗂️

Clear Sections

Use Contact | Education | Experience | Skills. Simple fonts (Arial/Calibri 10-12pt).

📇

Contact at Top

Name (large), Phone, Email, LinkedIn, City. No full address or photo needed.

🎓

Education First

For students: University/Degree | Expected Graduation | Relevant Coursework.

✍️

Content Tips

💪

Action Verbs

Start bullets with "Developed", "Led", "Analyzed", "Created" — not "Responsible for".

📊

Quantify Achievements

"Improved efficiency 30%" beats "Helped team". Use numbers and project counts.

🎯

Tailor Skills

Match job description keywords. List 6–8 skills: Python, Excel, Teamwork, etc.

🛠️

Projects & Courses

Add school projects, GitHub, hackathons as "Experience" if you have no jobs yet.

🤖

ATS Optimization

🏷️

Standard Headings

Use "Education", not "My School Years". Avoid tables and graphics.

🔑

Keywords from JD

Copy exact phrases from the job description, such as "data analysis".

📁

PDF Format

Always submit as PDF. Name it: FirstName_LastName_Internship.pdf.

✅

No Errors

Spellcheck everything. Get a professor or friend to review it.

⚠️

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Generic objective statement — just delete it
  • Irrelevant high school jobs (3+ years old)
  • Fancy design or graphics (ATS breaks them)
  • Typos and grammar errors
  • Listing duties instead of achievements
