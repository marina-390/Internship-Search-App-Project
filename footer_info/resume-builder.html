📝 CV Tips for Students

Essential advice to make your resume stand out for internships

Basic Structure

Keep it 1 Page

Focus on most relevant info for internships. Recruiters spend ~7 seconds scanning.

Clear Sections

Use Contact | Education | Experience | Skills. Simple fonts (Arial/Calibri 10-12pt).

Contact at Top

Name (large), Phone, Email, LinkedIn, City. No full address/photo needed.

Education First

For students: University/Degree | Expected Graduation | Relevant Coursework.

Content Tips

Action Verbs

Start bullets: "Developed", "Led", "Analyzed", "Created" (not "Responsible for").

Quantify Achievements

"Improved efficiency 30%" > "Helped team". Use numbers/projects.

Tailor Skills

Match job description keywords. List 6-8: Python, Excel, Teamwork, etc.

Projects & Courses

Add school projects, GitHub, hackathons as "Experience" if no jobs.

ATS Optimization

Standard Headings

Use "Education", not "My School Years". Avoid tables/graphics.

Keywords from JD

Copy exact phrases from job description (e.g. "data analysis").

PDF Format

Always PDF. Name: FirstName_LastName_Internship.pdf.

No Errors

Spellcheck everything. Get professor/friend review.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Generic objective ("seeking position") – delete it
  • Irrelevant high school jobs (3+ years old)
  • Fancy design/graphics (ATS breaks them)
  • Typos/grammar errors
  • Listing duties instead of achievements
